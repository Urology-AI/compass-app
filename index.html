What Is COMPASS?

COMPASS predicts surgical outcomes for prostate cancer patients by combining clinical data with three imaging modalities: MRI, micro-ultrasound (ExactVu), and PSMA PET/CT. It generates side-specific nerve-sparing recommendations and zone-level risk heatmaps for surgical planning.

Developed on 4,537 patients. Independently validated on 815 consecutive patients with trimodal imaging (682 original + 133 expanded; zero overlap with training). Side-specific ECE model validated on 409 lobes with true ipsilateral biopsy data.

PLND Decision Module

Based on N=664 consecutive RARP + PLND + PSMA PET patients. Implements an asymmetric decision rule derived from risk-stratified diagnostic accuracy analysis.

ScenarioLNI RateRecommendation
Non-HR + PSMA LN−0%Consider omitting PLND (zero false negatives)
Non-HR + PSMA LN+LowLimited PLND (low PPV, most are FP)
HR + PSMA LN−12%Always ePLND (12% occult LNI)
HR + PSMA LN+HighestAlways ePLND, high priority

NCCN High-Risk = GG ≥ 4 or PSA > 20 ng/mL. All 20 false negatives in the full cohort were NCCN high-risk patients.

Zone-Level Heatmap: csPCa Risk

The heatmap displays clinically significant prostate cancer (csPCa) risk per zone — the likelihood that each anatomical region harbors disease warranting treatment, not merely any cancer.

Definition of csPCa

GuidelinecsPCa Definition
AUA/SUO (2023)Grade Group ≥ 2 (Gleason ≥ 3+4)
NCCN (v5.2025)Grade Group ≥ 2, or GG1 with high volume (multiple cores, >50% involvement)
EAU (2024)ISUP Grade ≥ 2, or any grade with core length >6mm
PI-RADS v2.1PI-RADS 3-5 target csPCa detection (GG ≥ 2)

COMPASS uses GG ≥ 2 as the primary csPCa threshold, consistent with all major guidelines. Zone-level risk incorporates imaging suspicion (PI-RADS, PRI-MUS, SUV) as surrogates for csPCa probability at each location.

Known Limitations

• Single institution (Mount Sinai). External validation in progress.
• PSM depends heavily on surgical technique.
• BCR follow-up is maturing (60% complete).
• Predictions are decision support, not substitutes for clinical judgment.
